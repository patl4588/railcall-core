Governance, Risk & Compliance GRC

Do you hold SOC 2, ISO 27001, or equivalent certification?
ROADMAP
Not yet. SOC 2 Type II is in progress — controls mapped, audit underway. We do not display a badge we haven't earned. The report will be available under NDA once issued. ISO 27001 / 42001 are post-pilot milestones.
Do you maintain documented security policies?
DOCUMENTED
Yes — access, data-handling, incident-response, and vulnerability-disclosure policies are documented. Formal, independently-audited ISMS certification is in progress.
How do you handle regulatory compliance claims?
YES
Honestly. RailCall does not auto-grant you SOC 2 / HIPAA / GDPR. UNKNOWN means unverified — never a pass. We provide architectural boundaries + signed cryptographic evidence to support your own audit.

Data Security & Lifecycle DSI

What customer data do you store or process?
N/A
Your business data never reaches us. RailCall executes on the customer's own machine (127.0.0.1). We hold only account metadata: your email, the SHA-256 hash of your API key (never the raw key at rest), and your flow balance. No customer records, connector payloads, prompts (BYOK), outputs, schemas, or files.
Exactly what leaves the customer's machine?
YES — disclosed
Three things, named in full: (1) your business data — never, on any path; (2) the plain-English build prompt — a zero-retention proxy through our gateway to the model provider on the hosted engine, or straight from your machine to your own provider on BYOK (we never see it); (3) a blind billing ping — the hashed key + a nonce, no data. Verifiable on the wire and in every receipt as external_sockets: 0 during compute.
Do you use customer data to train models?
N/A
We can't — we never receive it. With BYOK, prompts go to your provider under your own no-train / zero-retention terms; the hosted engine is proxy-only and zero-retention.
Data classification & segregation?
N/A
There is no multi-tenant customer datastore to segregate — customer data lives only on each customer's own machine.

Encryption & Key Management EKM

Encryption in transit?
YES
TLS 1.2+ on the billing/account API and on the hosted build proxy to the model provider.
Encryption at rest & key custody?
YES
Provider keys and the Ed25519 signing seed live in a local 0600 owner-only vault on the customer's disk — the customer custodies them; we never hold them. Our account database (email, key-hash, balance) is encrypted at rest by the managed provider.
Do you have access to customer secrets?
N/A
No. Keys are BYOK, vaulted locally, used in-process, never transmitted to us. We cannot lose or abuse keys we never receive.

Identity & Access Management IAM

SSO / SAML / SCIM?
ROADMAP
Not yet — said plainly. When shipped, basic SAML will not sit behind an "SSO tax." Today, because the runtime is self-hosted and loopback-only, the first line of access control is the customer's own OS boundary; the Studio binds every mutating endpoint to a per-launch session token. For a pilot, we'll scope identity with your team.
Account authentication & password storage?
YES
PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256, per-user random salt, constant-time verification. Plaintext passwords are never stored or logged.
Role-based access control?
YES
Organizations, members, and roles are supported; org isolation is structural (a caller's key resolves to its org — nothing to forge).
MFA on the vendor account?
ROADMAP
Account MFA is on the roadmap. Note the security-critical boundary is local: the runtime and secrets live behind the customer's own machine auth, not a shared cloud login.

Application & Interface Security AIS

How is the local runtime protected from other software on the machine?
YES
Loopback-only binding, a per-launch CSRF session token on every mutating endpoint, no-Origin request rejection, and input validation that refuses malformed keys before any socket opens.
Is generated/composed code safe?
YES
The Builder composes strictly from a pre-audited, license-screened library — never freehand code. Unresolved pieces are flagged needs-audit, never faked as audited. A deterministic snap-to-library keeps verdicts stable run-to-run.
Injection / adversarial resistance?
YES
A per-connector gate suite tests input-sanitization and injection; prompt-injection attempts and fake-authority overrides are refused (independently drill-tested — an embedded "SYSTEM OVERRIDE / admin team" prompt got zero traction).

Infrastructure & Network Security IVS

Where is the vendor infrastructure hosted?
YES
The account/billing gateway runs on a managed cloud provider (Render, USA) with an encrypted managed Postgres. The data plane — everything touching your systems — runs on your hardware, not ours.
Network egress from the customer machine?
YES — measured
Measured by an lsof sweep, not asserted: external_sockets: 0 during governed compute. The only egress is the disclosed billing ping and (if used) the hosted build proxy.
Admin/dashboard routes exposed publicly?
YES — disabled
Admin routes are gated by an env flag that is not set on the public gateway (they 404 on the internet) and only function on a local operator machine.

Threat & Vulnerability Management TVM

Do you run penetration tests?
ROADMAP
An independent third-party pen-test is planned before serious enterprise rollout. Today: structural self-audit + a deterministic gate suite + the tamper-evidence in every signed receipt. We will not imply an external pen-test exists until one does.
Dependency / supply-chain integrity?
YES
The composed library is license-screened and version-pinned; the shipped desktop app is code-signed + notarized; build integrity is folded into a sha256 root in every receipt.
Responsible disclosure?
YES
security@railcall.ai, and a machine-readable policy at /.well-known/security.txt (RFC 9116).

Security Incident Management SEF

Documented incident-response process?
DOCUMENTED
Detect → contain → assess → notify affected account holders without undue delay → remediate → post-mortem. Because we hold no customer data, any incident on our side is bounded to account metadata (email, key-hash, balance).
Breach-notification commitment?
YES
We notify affected account holders promptly. There is no customer business data on our side that a breach could expose.

Business Continuity & Resilience BCR

Availability / DR for the service?
YES — scoped
The gateway meters billing; its managed provider handles backups + failover. Crucially, your agents keep running with no vendor-side availability dependency — governed execution is local. Free-tier flows are enforced locally; a gateway outage does not stop your local work.
Can a customer recover their audit trail after a machine loss?
YES
railcall backup bundles receipts + the hash-chained policy history into a portable, self-verifying archive (secrets excluded); railcall restore verifies every byte before writing. Machine loss costs the hardware, not the compliance record.

Supply Chain & Subprocessors STA

List your subprocessors.
YES — public
Render (host + Postgres for account/billing), Stripe (payments — card details never touch RailCall), Groq (hosted build engine, zero-retention, only when the hosted engine is used — BYOK avoids it), Resend (account email when enabled). None receive your business data. Full list + notes on the Trust page.
Change notification for subprocessors?
YES
We notify account holders before adding a subprocessor that changes the data posture.

AI-Specific Controls AI

No-training guarantee on prompts/outputs?
YES
With BYOK, prompts flow to your provider under your own contract; we never intermediate them. The hosted engine is proxy-only, zero-retention. No RailCall-side prompt/output retention.
Agent identity & attribution?
YES
Every governed action is attributed in a signed receipt to a human approval or a named policy version (policy_auto (v2)) — never an anonymous agent.
Kill switch / human-in-the-loop?
YES
Dry-run + approval-gated by default. Four un-editable hard floors always stop irreversible actions, unclassified actions, policy changes, and unpriced spend for a human. One-click revert-to-strictest. Actions are classed reversible / compensable / irreversible; irreversible can never auto-fire.
Framework alignment (NIST AI RMF / EU AI Act)?
DOCUMENTED
Designed toward NIST AI RMF (approval thresholds, reversibility classification, kill switch, full action-history receipts). The signed, hash-chained receipts give a deployer the per-action audit trail the EU AI Act's logging obligations call for. We map to your controls; we don't grant compliance.
