The governed loop · ● live

Four floors. Every write crosses one.

Writing to your codebase, shipping a change, moving money, messaging a customer — every irreversible action passes all four gates. No policy can widen a floor without itself being a signed action.

plan → approve → prove
F1 · HARD FLOOR

BYOK vault

Keys live in a local 0600 vault, resolved only at 127.0.0.1. Nothing authenticates over a network you don't own.

F2 · AIRLOCK

Dry-run first

Every action is computed as a signed preview. Zero writes until you've seen the exact diff.

F3 · APPROVAL

Human sign-off

Keypair-based, terminal-only — the browser never sees it. The drafting surface can't approve itself.

F4 · PROOF

Signed receipt

Every approved action mints an Ed25519 receipt with an integrity hash and full action log. Verify offline.

🔒 No bypass. A missing signature, a stale plan, or a global freeze all fail closed — nothing fires.

Built for regulated work ● Independent HIPAA audit — in progress

Why we believe local-first is the right shape for HIPAA.

We built RailCall so protected health information never has to leave your infrastructure. The data plane is loopback — PHI is read, transformed, and governed at 127.0.0.1, and RailCall itself never receives it. That design is why we believe a local-first deployment is well-positioned for HIPAA-regulated work, and it's why we're putting it through an independent audit even though a local-only tool arguably wouldn't require one. The result is that HIPAA's technical safeguards stop being paperwork and become something you can point at.

§164.312(a)

Access control

BYOK keys in a 0600 vault, resolved only on-box; every consequential action needs a terminal-only keypair approval the browser never sees.

§164.312(b)

Audit controls

Every action emits an Ed25519-signed, hash-chained receipt. Tamper any past entry and the chain breaks — evidence, not a log you have to trust.

§164.312(c)

Integrity

The approved bytes provably equal the fired bytes. Drift, a stale plan, or a bad signature fail closed — nothing executes.

§164.312(e)

Transmission

The highest-risk transfer — PHI leaving your network — simply doesn't happen. What reaches a model is your choice, under your own BAA.

Straight scope: HIPAA compliance is a posture of the covered entity, not a checkbox on any vendor's product — so we don't claim to make you compliant. We reduce your surface, produce the evidence, and are validating that position under an independent audit. We make compliance provable, not just asserted.
