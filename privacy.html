Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 26, 2026 · Last updated: June 26, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how AiTrade ("AiTrade," "RailCall," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares information in connection with the RailCall website, the RailCall Studio application, our command-line tools, the metering gateway, and related services (collectively, the "Service"). RailCall is local-first by design: your workflow data, files, provider keys, and generated code never leave your machine. We collect only the limited information described below to run your account and meter usage. By using the Service, you agree to this Policy.

1. Introduction

AiTrade is the controller of the personal information processed under this Policy. This Policy applies to information we process as part of operating the Service. It does not apply to third-party services you choose to connect through RailCall's bring-your-own-key ("BYOK") model, or to third-party websites we link to, each of which has its own privacy practices. Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings given in our Terms of Service.

2. Information we collect

We collect only a limited set of information needed to provide the Service:

  • Account email — the email address you provide to create an account, used to identify your account, sign you in, and communicate with you about the Service.
  • Password hash — we never store your plaintext password. We store only a salted PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 hash used to authenticate you.
  • API-key hash — your API key is stored at rest only as a SHA-256 hash, used to validate your flows against your balance. We do not retain your API key in plaintext.
  • Billing identifiers — when you purchase flows, our payment processor (Stripe) assigns a customer identifier and related billing tokens. We receive and store these identifiers and confirmation of payment so we can associate purchases with your account. We do not receive or store your full payment-card number.
  • Run & usage counts — counts of governed flows metered through the gateway and basic account/plan state, used to maintain your balance, enforce limits, and bill usage.
  • Support messages — any information you choose to share with us when you contact us for support through Discord at railcall.ai/discord.
  • Minimal server logs — limited technical logs (such as request timestamps, hashed-key references, and error codes) generated by the gateway to keep the metering and authentication endpoints running and secure.

3. Information we do NOT collect / never receive

This is the core of RailCall. Because the Service is local-first, the following never leave your device and are never transmitted to us: your workflow data and the operational data your workflows process; your source files and local folders; your provider and API keys (kept in a local 0600 vault); and your generated code and artifacts. When the gateway meters a flow, the client transmits only a one-way hash of your API key plus a single-use nonce — enough to verify your balance and record the flow, and nothing more. The gateway is a transaction register, not a data sink: it is not designed to receive, and does not receive, your raw keys, files, data, or code.

4. How we use information

  • To create, authenticate, and maintain your account and to provision your API key.
  • To process payments through Stripe and to meter, count, and bill governed flows.
  • To maintain your prepaid balance and enforce free-tier and plan limits.
  • To provide support and respond to your requests.
  • To operate, secure, debug, and improve the metering and authentication endpoints, and to detect and prevent fraud or abuse of the metering system.
  • To send you service-related communications about your account, security, or material changes to the Service or this Policy.
  • To comply with our legal obligations and to enforce our Terms.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use your operational or workflow data to train models — and as explained above, that data never reaches us in the first place.

5. Legal bases for processing (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or another region whose law requires a legal basis for processing, we rely on the following bases:

  • Performance of a contract — to create and operate your account, provision your API key, meter flows, and process the purchases you request (Article 6(1)(b)).
  • Legitimate interests — to secure the Service, prevent fraud and abuse of metering, debug, and improve our endpoints, balanced against your rights and interests (Article 6(1)(f)).
  • Legal obligation — to meet tax, accounting, and other legal requirements (Article 6(1)(c)).
  • Consent — where we ask for it, for example any optional communications; you may withdraw consent at any time (Article 6(1)(a)).

6. Cookies & local storage

The public RailCall website is built to run without third-party advertising or tracking cookies. We use only essential, first-party storage needed to operate the Service. In particular, after you sign in, your API key may be saved in your browser's localStorage on your own device so you do not have to re-enter it; this stays in your browser and is not a tracking cookie. We do not run third-party advertising networks on the Service. You can clear local storage through your browser settings at any time, though doing so may require you to sign in again.

7. Sub-processors

We share limited information with the following sub-processors, each only to the extent needed for its stated purpose and under contracts that limit their use of the data:

  • Stripe — processes payments for flow purchases and holds your billing/payment information.
  • Groq — provides LLM inference for the cascade when you enable it.
  • Discord — hosts our community and support channel where you may contact us.
  • Render — hosts the metering gateway that authenticates accounts and counts flows.
  • GitHub — hosts and delivers the static RailCall website via GitHub Pages.

We do not sell or rent personal information. We may also disclose information where required by law or legal process, to protect rights, safety, or the integrity of the Service, or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

8. Data retention

We retain account information (such as your email and credential hashes), billing identifiers, and flow/usage counts for as long as your account is active and as needed to provide the Service. After account closure, we retain records only as long as necessary to comply with our legal, tax, and accounting obligations, to resolve disputes, and to enforce our agreements, after which we delete or anonymize them. Minimal server logs are kept for a limited period for security and debugging. Because your keys, files, workflow data, and generated code remain on your device, we do not retain that material at all.

9. Security measures

We use technical and organizational measures appropriate to the limited data we hold. Passwords are stored as salted PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 hashes; API keys are stored at rest only as SHA-256 hashes; data is encrypted in transit; and the metering and authentication endpoints are strictly scoped. Receipts are signed locally on your machine with ed25519 keys. Because your most sensitive material — your keys, files, workflow data, and generated code — stays on your device in a local 0600 vault and never reaches us, it is never in our custody. No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure, and you are responsible for safeguarding your own machine, credentials, and keys.

10. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights over your personal information. We honor applicable rights and will not discriminate against you for exercising them.

If you are in the EEA or UK (GDPR/UK GDPR), you may have the right to: access the personal information we hold about you; have inaccurate information rectified; request erasure ("right to be forgotten"); receive a copy of certain information in a portable format; and object to or restrict certain processing. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority.

If you are a California resident (CCPA/CPRA), you may have the right to: know what personal information we collect, use, and disclose; request deletion of your personal information; opt out of any sale or sharing of personal information (we do not sell or share your personal information as those terms are defined); and not be discriminated against for exercising your rights.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@railcall.ai. We may need to verify your identity before acting on your request, and some records may be retained where the law requires it.

We and our sub-processors are based in, or process information in, the United States. If you access the Service from outside the United States, you understand that your information may be transferred to, stored in, and processed in the United States and other countries whose data-protection laws may differ from those in your jurisdiction. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards for such transfers, such as the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses, and we limit the information transferred to the minimal account, billing, and usage data described in this Policy.

12. Children's privacy

If you believe a child under 16 has provided us with personal information, please contact us at privacy@railcall.ai and we will take steps to delete it.
