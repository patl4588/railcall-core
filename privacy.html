Privacy Policy

Effective: June 21, 2026 · Last updated: June 21, 2026

RailCall is local-first by design. The short version: your operational data, source files, API keys, and workflow contents stay on your machine. We collect only what we need to run your account, license your seat, and meter usage.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy explains how RailCall, a service operated by AiTrade ("AiTrade," "RailCall," "we," "us," or "our"), handles information in connection with the RailCall website, the RailCall Studio application, the command-line tools, and related services (together, the "Service"). By using the Service you agree to this Policy.

2. Information we collect

We collect a limited set of information:

  • Account & identity data — the email address and credentials you provide to create an account and activate a seat. Passwords are stored only as salted hashes.
  • Billing data — handled by our third-party payment processor. We receive confirmation of payment and a token; we do not store full card numbers.
  • Usage & metering data — counts of metered runs and basic license state, used to bill usage and enforce your plan.
  • Support data — information you send us when you contact support.
  • Minimal server logs — limited technical logs (such as request timestamps and error codes) needed to keep the identity and licensing endpoints running and secure.

3. What stays on your machine (and never reaches us)

This is the core of RailCall. The following never leave your device and are never transmitted to us: your API keys (stored in a local 0600 vault), your source files and local folders, your workflow contents and generated artifacts, your audit receipts and logs, and any operational data processed by your workflows. After activation, operational traffic flows only between your machine and your own BYOK (bring-your-own-key) providers.

4. How we use information

  • To create and authenticate your account and activate your seat/license.
  • To process payments and meter usage.
  • To provide support and respond to your requests.
  • To maintain the security, integrity, and reliability of the identity and licensing endpoints.
  • To comply with legal obligations.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use your operational or workflow data to train models.

5. How we share information

We share information only with:

  • Service providers who process data on our behalf (for example, our payment processor and hosting/identity infrastructure), under contracts that limit their use of the data.
  • Legal & safety — where required by law, legal process, or to protect rights, safety, and the integrity of the Service.
  • Business transfers — in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

We do not sell or rent personal information to third parties.

6. Cookies & analytics

The public RailCall website is built to run without third-party trackers or advertising cookies. We may use a minimal, first-party session mechanism strictly to keep you signed in to your account. We do not run third-party advertising networks on the Service.

7. Data retention

We retain account, billing, and metering records for as long as your account is active and as needed to comply with legal, tax, and accounting obligations. You may request deletion of your account as described below; some records may be retained where the law requires it.

8. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, export, or delete the personal information we hold about you, and to object to or restrict certain processing. To exercise these rights, contact us at the address below. We honor applicable rights under laws such as the GDPR and the CCPA/CPRA. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

9. Security

We use technical and organizational measures appropriate to the limited data we hold, including encryption in transit and strict scoping of the identity/licensing endpoints. Because keys and operational data remain on your device in a local 0600 vault, the most sensitive material is never in our custody. No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure, and you are responsible for safeguarding your own machine, credentials, and keys.

10. Children

The Service is intended for developers and is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

11. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. We will post the updated version here and revise the "Last updated" date. Material changes will be communicated through the Service or by email where appropriate.

12. Governing law

This Policy and any dispute arising out of it are governed by the laws of the State of Florida, United States, without regard to its conflict-of-laws rules. See our Terms of Service for venue and dispute-resolution terms.
