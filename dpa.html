Data Processing Agreement

entity: AiTrade · plain-English summary · the executable DPA + SCCs are provided on request

This is a plain-English summary of how a Data Processing Agreement works with RailCall. Because RailCall is local-first, the vendor processes almost no personal data — which makes for an unusually short and clean DPA. The binding document (with EU Standard Contractual Clauses where relevant) is available on request.

The short version: your business data and any personal data in your workflows never reach us — they stay on your machine. The only personal data RailCall processes as a "processor" is minimal account metadata. So the DPA covers a tiny surface.

Standard DPA terms (as applied to RailCall)

  • Purpose limitation: account metadata is used only to run your account, meter usage, and provide support — nothing else.
  • Confidentiality: personnel with access are bound by confidentiality obligations.
  • Security: encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest for the account database; keys are customer-custodied locally (see the Trust page).
  • Subprocessors: Render, Stripe, Groq (hosted engine only), Resend — listed publicly; change-notification before any that alters the data posture.
  • International transfers: covered by EU Standard Contractual Clauses where applicable. Your customer data is processed on your own hardware, so there is no vendor-side cross-border transfer of it.
  • Data-subject requests: we assist you in responding; account data is deletable on request.
  • Sub-processing & audit: reasonable audit/information rights on request, consistent with a low-data-surface processor.
  • Deletion / return: on termination, account metadata is deleted; your local artifacts remain yours.
Legal note. This page is a plain-English summary for evaluation, not the binding agreement and not legal advice. The executable DPA (with SCCs) is provided on request and is being finalized with counsel. To request it or ask a question, email legal@railcall.ai or security@railcall.ai.
