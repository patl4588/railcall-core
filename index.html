Honest Scope

Railcall makes no blanket regulatory exemption claim—it does not declare you SOC2 / HIPAA / GDPR compliant, "0% risk," or exempt from review. Local-by-design reduces the surface; your regulatory posture remains yours to determine.

No fake green. RailCall reports state honestly: UNKNOWN means unverified — not a pass. A receipt asserts only what was measured; anything unproven is surfaced as unknown, never shown as a false success. SOC2 / HIPAA / GDPR are not automatically granted by using RailCall.

Safe by default: RailCall runs locally in dry-run / proof mode — no live sends, charges, USDC settlement, or card billing happen unless you turn them on. Payment tracks are forward-looking.

How we costed the comparison. Scenario: a small team builds and runs ten agentic workflows over one month. Build-on-Claude side — a real agentic build re-sends millions of tokens of context across many turns; at Claude's published Opus 4.8 list rates ($5 / M input, $25 / M output) one build runs ≈ $20–40, so ten ≈ $300. Freehand code drifts, so ~3 rebuilds ≈ $90. Each of the 1,000 runs reasons through the model again ≈ $250. Auditing and license-checking the output is your own time. Total ≈ $640. RailCall side — workflows are composed from a pre-audited, license-screened library and metered at a flat $0.005 per governed run: ~200 runs to compose ten ($1), no token-metered regeneration because drift is held by a signed integrity root ($0), 1,000 runs ($5), audit included. Total ≈ $6. These are illustrative estimates at published list prices — real spend varies with workload size, prompt caching, model choice, and how often the code drifts.
