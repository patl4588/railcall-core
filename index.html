Hybrid Automation Overlay

Bring your own plumbing.
We provide the guardrails.

Keep your n8n, Zapier, and custom pipelines — they're great at the chaotic public web: raw APIs, 400+ SaaS apps. The moment a workflow needs an AI decision or code that touches something that matters, dump that payload into RailCall — the local, loopback-first control plane that turns drift-prone streams into governed, receipted action.

01 · DUMP

Your data stays on your disk

Your workflow POSTs its raw payload into RailCall's local loopback gate (127.0.0.1:8799). It lands in a governed local log and stays there — no cloud, no third party. A model is only ever called with your own key, on exactly what you choose to send.

02 · LOCK

Govern every action at the Airlock

RailCall validates against an audited library with a deterministic compile. Anything unaudited — or any write or send — halts at the Approval Airlock. You see the exact payload and explicitly approve it. Read-only and dry-run by default.

03 · RUN

A receipt you can't fake

Execution runs locally. Every run emits a hash-sealed receipt (sha256 integrity, re-verified by an independent auditor). No green checkmark unless the artifact on disk actually matches. No fake green.

Honest Scope

Railcall makes no blanket regulatory exemption claim—it does not declare you SOC2 / HIPAA / GDPR compliant, "0% risk," or exempt from review. Local-by-design reduces the surface; your regulatory posture remains yours to determine.

No fake green. RailCall reports state honestly: UNKNOWN means unverified — not a pass. A receipt asserts only what was measured; anything unproven is surfaced as unknown, never shown as a false success. SOC2 / HIPAA / GDPR are not automatically granted by using RailCall.

Safe by default: RailCall runs locally in dry-run / proof mode — no live sends, charges, USDC settlement, or card billing happen unless you turn them on. Payment tracks are forward-looking.

How we costed the comparison. Scenario: a small team builds and runs ten agentic workflows over one month. Freehand code drifts, so ~3 rebuilds ≈ $90. Each of the 1,000 runs reasons through the model again ≈ $250. Auditing and license-checking the output is your own time. Total ≈ $640. Total ≈ $6. These are illustrative estimates at published list prices — real spend varies with workload size, prompt caching, model choice, and how often the code drifts.
