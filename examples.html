What the receipt proves

ed25519-signed receipt key_id 14b9f6ef93c38c8f — the QA station's signing key, pinned in its workspace receipt chain intact external_sockets: 0 0 unaudited pieces dry-run · approval-held

Every step on the receipt resolves to an audited component from the pre-coded library — the approval hold is combo:agent_contract_broker, the ledger post is combo:api_fetch_validated. These ids ship with the Studio install (grep them yourself under ~/.railcall/station/library/ after installing) — they are not in the public site repo. RailCall never invents a connector to make a build look finished.

Why this one matters: it's money-touching automation with the default you actually want — the irreversible action is held for a human (require_human policy on every write), and the signed receipt proves exactly what was staged, on your disk, checkable without contacting anyone.

Example 02 · scheduled report

Every morning: aggregate the numbers, post the summary

What the user typed

plain-English recipedaily report: schedule trigger → aggregate metrics → post summary to Discord (dry-run)

What RailCall built

Trigger — daily schedule
↓
pure calculationAggregate metrics — computed locally, no network
↓
dry-runPost summary — outbound message, staged until approved

What the receipt proves

ed25519-signed receipt, persisted to disk aggregation step: no network dry-run — the receipt says so itself

Connectors resolved to audited library components: combo:api_fetch_validated for the fetch and outbound_communications for the post, with the aggregation as a pure-calculation node.

Why this one matters: it's the everyday shape any developer recognizes — cron → aggregate → notify — built on the live station and honestly labeled. The receipt self-reports dry-run rather than pretending a staged message was delivered. That's the point: RailCall would rather show you an honest hold than a fake green light.

What these receipts claim — and what they don't. Each receipt is signed, verifiable, and approval-held: it proves what was composed, that every piece came from the audited pre-coded library, and that no writes happened. It does not claim the flow "passed" — RailCall deliberately withholds that verdict until a human approves the staged actions. A dry-run receipt that honestly says dry-run is worth more than a green badge you can't check.

The proof works the same way on your machine

The properties above aren't special to our QA station — they're how every RailCall build works:

Loopback only
The Studio runs on 127.0.0.1. Your records and drafts stay on your machine.
BYOK vault
Your provider keys live in a local vault. They are never uploaded.
Blind metering
Billing counts flows — a hash and a nonce. No payloads, no content, ever.
Receipts verify offline
Run railcall verify <receipt.json> — the Ed25519 signature checks against the public key embedded in the receipt itself. No server, no trust in us. In the Studio: PROOF rail → VERIFY ALL FROM DISK.
