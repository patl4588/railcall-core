What is Local-First AI Automation?

Local-first AI automation refers to the practice of running AI-powered automation workflows on the user's own machine, rather than relying on cloud-based services. This approach ensures that sensitive data remains on the user's device, reducing the risk of data breaches and unauthorized access. By keeping data local, users can maintain control and security over their information.

Benefits of Local-First AI Automation

The primary benefit of local-first AI automation is enhanced security and privacy. By keeping data on the user's machine, the risk of data breaches and unauthorized access is significantly reduced. Additionally, local-first AI automation provides users with full control over their data, allowing them to make decisions about how their information is used and shared. This approach also enables users to work offline, making it ideal for situations where internet connectivity is limited or unreliable.

How Local-First AI Automation Works

Local-first AI automation typically involves installing software on the user's machine, which then runs automated workflows using AI-powered tools. The software is designed to operate offline, with all processing and data storage occurring on the user's device. This approach ensures that data never leaves the user's machine, providing an additional layer of security and privacy. In the case of RailCall, the software uses a governed program that runs on the user's own machine, with every run sealed with an Ed25519 receipt verified offline.
